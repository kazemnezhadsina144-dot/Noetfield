Angel / strategic convertible round
Angel Convertible

A small, governance-aligned round for Noetfield.

Noetfield is opening a focused 25–50k CAD angel / strategic convertible round via Cenatune – the holding and capital spine – to fund a 6-week Noetfield Trust Brief pilot, the initial Trust Ledger product, and core legal and infrastructure work. The structure keeps Noetfield’s cap table clean while giving early backers discounted exposure to future equity.

Context

What this round is funding

Real-economy AI governance

Noetfield Systems Inc. is building an AI governance and “trust telemetry” layer for organisations that need to use AI in a defensible, documented way in front of boards, regulators, and stakeholders.

The first product is a 6-week Noetfield Trust Brief pilot and an ongoing Trust Ledger reporting system that turns scattered AI artefacts into a board-ready record of critical AI decisions, safeguards, and accountability.

This initial angel / strategic round is deliberately small. The goal is not to pre-empt a seed round, but to concretely fund:

  • Packaging and delivery of the AI Governance & Trust Playbook and Trust Brief.
  • 2–3 design-partner pilots in real-economy, regulated contexts.
  • Core legal, accounting, and infrastructure to keep Noetfield investor-ready.
In one sentence: this round funds a small number of real pilots and a clean, board-ready AI governance product – not a marketing machine.
Headline structure
Angel / strategic convertible via Cenatune

Capital is raised at Cenatune, the holding / capital spine. Cenatune then lends that capital into Noetfield Systems Inc. as an intercompany loan to fund product, pilots, and core infrastructure.

This structure keeps Noetfield’s cap table clean for future institutional rounds and groups early backers into a single, governance-aligned instrument.

Terms

Proposed convertible terms (high level)

Unsecured · high risk

Final details are set out in the formal Convertible Loan Agreement; this is a clear, human-readable summary.

  • Issuer: Cenatune (holding / capital spine).
  • Instrument: unsecured convertible loan.
  • Round size: approximately 25–50k CAD total.
  • Minimum ticket: from 5k CAD.
  • Interest: 7% simple annual interest, no monthly payments (interest accrues).
  • Maturity: 36 months (3 years).
  • Conversion: automatic conversion of principal and accrued interest into the same class of shares as future institutional investors at a 20% discount in a priced round.
  • If no round by maturity: repay principal plus interest, or agree to convert into a small equity stake at a fair valuation at that time.
  • Security: unsecured (no collateral).
What this means in practice
You are not buying common stock today; you are extending a high-risk loan that can become equity later.
If Noetfield completes a priced round, you convert into equity at a discount to those investors.
If there is no qualified round by maturity, we either repay you or agree a small, direct equity position.
This is designed to be clean for future VC / strategic capital and simple for early backers.
Risk profile

Risk and upside – how to think about this

0–multiple-X outcome

This is high-risk early-stage capital. It is not a fixed-income product and not a savings instrument. You should assume you could lose 100% of your investment.

The upside comes only if Noetfield:

  • Successfully delivers Trust Brief pilots to real operators under real regulatory pressure.
  • Establishes the Trust Ledger as a credible governance artefact for boards and regulators.
  • Raises institutional capital at higher valuations or achieves a strategic exit.

In that scenario, a 10k CAD loan that ultimately converts into roughly 0.5% equity could, for example, be worth ~100k CAD at a 20M CAD company valuation. This is an illustration, not a forecast or guarantee; the real outcome range is from 0 to that order of magnitude.

Use of proceeds

Where the money actually goes

Focused deployment

Proceeds from this round are earmarked for a small number of tangible milestones, not broad, unfocused spend:

  • Finalising and packaging the AI Governance & Trust Playbook.
  • Delivering 2–3 Noetfield Trust Brief pilots with design partners.
  • Establishing a minimal but robust legal and accounting baseline for future investment.
Not included
No mass marketing or performance-ad spend.
No large, fixed-cost engineering hires at this stage.
No unrelated side products or speculative spin-outs.
Fit

Who this is suitable for

This structure is intended for angels and strategic backers who:

  • Understand early-stage technology and governance risk.
  • Are comfortable with high volatility and illiquidity.
  • Prefer a clean, governance-first structure over a noisy cap table.
  • See value in an AI governance and Trust Ledger layer for real-economy operators.

If you prefer guaranteed or low-risk returns, this is not the right instrument.
