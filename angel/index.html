What Noetfield is

AI governance & trust telemetry for real-economy boards

Governance-first

Noetfield Systems Inc. is building an AI governance and trust telemetry layer for organisations whose AI usage must stand up to boards, regulators, and institutional investors – not only to product teams.

The initial focus is two artefacts:

  • Noetfield Trust Brief – a 6-week engagement that maps where AI is truly material, identifies high-impact decisions, and delivers a board-ready governance brief.
  • Noetfield Trust Ledger – a concise, board-level record of critical AI decisions, safeguards, and accountability, designed to sit above existing technical and risk controls.

Both are deliberately narrow and governance-first: they do not compete with model vendors or cloud platforms. Instead, they make AI usage legible, defensible, and traceable to the people with fiduciary duties.

Capital structure
How capital flows into Noetfield

Capital is raised through Cenatune, the holding and capital spine for Noetfield. Cenatune then lends that capital into Noetfield Systems Inc. as an intercompany loan to fund:

  • Product & engineering for the Trust Ledger infrastructure.
  • Initial Trust Brief pilots with real-economy operators.
  • Legal, risk, and accounting work to keep the structure clean.
  • Targeted board, regulator, and partner engagement.

This keeps the operating company’s cap table clean for future institutional rounds, with early backers grouped in a single, governable vehicle.

Instrument

Unsecured convertible loan via Cenatune

Clean & future-round compatible

A governance-aligned structure that gives downside clarity and upside participation, while keeping Noetfield’s operating cap table institutional-ready.

Instead of scattering small equity positions directly on Noetfield Systems Inc., eligible angels and strategic partners provide capital to Cenatune as an unsecured convertible loan. Cenatune then on-lends those funds to Noetfield as an intercompany loan.

  • Issuer: Cenatune – the holding / capital spine for Noetfield.
  • Use of funds: Cenatune lends into Noetfield Systems Inc. to fund product, pilots, and core infrastructure.
  • Form: unsecured convertible loan (not common equity, not a public bond).

This pattern respects early backers, keeps governance straightforward, and avoids boxing in future priced rounds with a fragmented early cap table.

Reference terms (illustrative)
Headline parameters
  • Round size: approximately 50k–250k CAD total.
  • Typical ticket: from 50k CAD for eligible angels or strategic partners.
  • Interest: simple annual interest (e.g. 7%), accrued, no monthly payments.
  • Maturity: 36 months from the date of the loan.
  • Conversion: automatic conversion into the same class of shares as a future priced equity round in Noetfield Systems Inc., at a discount to that round (e.g. 20%).
  • Security: unsecured (high-risk, early-stage capital).
  • Jurisdiction: British Columbia, Canada, under applicable prospectus exemptions (e.g. accredited or otherwise eligible investors under NI 45-106).

Exact terms, including any valuation parameters and discount, are confirmed in formal documentation and may vary by investor and round.

Risk & upside

How to think about the bet

This is high-risk early-stage capital. It should be treated as a venture-style bet, not as a savings product or guaranteed return.

  • You should assume you can lose 100% of the capital you commit.
  • There is no fixed profit, no guarantee of liquidity, and no promise of a future round or exit.
  • Any upside depends on Noetfield’s ability to grow, secure institutional mandates, and complete a priced equity round or exit.

FAQ

FAQ – structure, risk, and exemptions

Answer-ready
What exactly is Noetfield building?
Noetfield is focused on AI governance for real-economy boards and regulators. The Noetfield Trust Brief provides a 6-week governance pilot that maps material AI use-cases and produces a board-ready report. The Noetfield Trust Ledger then becomes a concise record of critical AI decisions, safeguards, and accountability, linked back to existing technical and risk controls.
Why raise through Cenatune instead of directly on Noetfield’s cap table?
Raising through Cenatune lets early backers be grouped in a single, governable vehicle while keeping Noetfield Systems Inc.’s cap table clean for future institutional rounds. It avoids a fragmented early shareholder register and supports a more straightforward path to future priced equity and regulatory scrutiny.
Is this structure compatible with future VC or strategic rounds?
Yes. The unsecured convertible loan is designed to convert into the same class of shares as a future priced round in Noetfield, at a discount to that round. The goal is to respect early backers while keeping terms straightforward and familiar for institutional investors and their counsel.
Is this a public solicitation or a prospectus?
No. This page is informational only. It is not a prospectus, and it is not a public offering of securities. Any investment will be made solely through formal documentation and under applicable prospectus exemptions (for example, accredited or otherwise eligible investors under NI 45-106 in British Columbia, Canada).
Does this replace legal, tax, or investment advice?
No. Nothing on this page is legal, tax, or investment advice. Prospective investors should obtain their own advice from qualified professionals and make decisions based on their own circumstances and risk tolerance.
How are terms finalised?
If there is mutual interest and a fit in mandate, Noetfield will share the detailed Angel Overview and proposed term sheet in writing. From there, terms are finalised through standard documentation (convertible loan agreement, board resolutions, and related materials) reviewed by counsel on both sides.
