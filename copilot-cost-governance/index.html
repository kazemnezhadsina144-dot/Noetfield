Context

Why this Copilot pack exists

Cost + risk drift

Copilot is not only a license line item. It expands the governance surface: new access patterns, new agents, new content flows, and new failure modes. Without a record, cost and risk simply drift.

  • Clarify Copilot scope (which experiences are in use, by whom, and for what).
  • Detect license waste, under-adoption, and misaligned spend.
  • Inventory Copilot agents, owners, and data scope in one place.
  • Reduce oversharing and exposure from orphaned or outdated sources.
  • Produce board-ready artefacts instead of scattered spreadsheets and chats.
Outcome: you know where Copilot is used, what it costs, what it touches, and which controls exist — in a format that survives audit and board scrutiny.
Definition

What the Copilot readiness pack is

Board-ready & governance-first

The Noetfield Copilot Cost & Governance Readiness pack is a focused engagement that turns a Copilot rollout — or planned rollout — into a documented decision. It brings together cost, scope, agents, data exposure, and controls into a short, auditable record.

It assumes Copilot is either already live in parts of the organisation, or about to be. The pack creates an inventory and governance view: who has what, which agents exist, what data paths matter, and how conditions for “proceed, pause, or scale” are framed.

The output is structured so that it can feed directly into Noetfield’s Trust Ledger and into your wider AI governance posture over time.

In one sentence: a 2–3 week Copilot-specific diagnostic that produces a cost map, agent inventory, exposure scan, and a board-ready decision memo plus Trust Ledger entries.

What you get

Concrete Copilot artefacts, not generic AI advice

The pack is designed to produce a small set of high-leverage artefacts that you can plug into existing governance, not another long slide deck.

Cost & scope
1) Copilot cost map

Clarifies Copilot types in use, license SKUs, and where spend can drift if adoption is uneven or ungoverned.

  • License types vs business units or functions.
  • Baseline spend and projected scenarios.
  • Waste and over-provisioning signals.
Utilization
2) License utilization report

A short utilization view: assigned vs active usage, adoption blockers, and a practical cleanup plan.

  • Low-usage clusters and patterns.
  • Areas where Copilot could replace legacy spend.
  • Signals for ongoing monitoring.
Agent layer
3) Agent inventory (MVP ledger)

A first-generation ledger of Copilot agents, with owners, purpose, data scope, and risk tier.

  • Who created what, and for which process.
  • Which sources each agent depends on.
  • Flags for high-sensitivity combinations.
Exposure
4) Oversharing and exposure scan

Short risk map of sensitive paths: oversharing, shadow data, and misaligned access patterns.

  • High-risk content and teams.
  • Common misconfigurations and quick fixes.
  • Recommendations for safe defaults.
Controls
5) Control baseline

A minimal, realistic governance baseline specific to Copilot usage.

  • Policy hooks and lifecycle rules.
  • Reporting cadence and owner assignments.
  • Links into broader AI governance and security.
Board artefact
6) Board decision memo + Trust Ledger entries

A short decision memo plus a small set of Copilot entries ready to drop into Noetfield’s Trust Ledger.

  • Proceed / pause / limited pilot / scale framing.
  • Explicit conditions and safeguards.
  • Ledger entries as ongoing evidence anchors.

Process

How the Copilot readiness work runs

Designed as a short diagnostic that respects existing tools and workloads. Most work is async and based on artefacts you already have.

  • 1) Intake (48h) – licensing context, target groups, existing governance constraints.
  • 2) Mapping – Copilot scope, license allocation, and first agent inventory.
  • 3) Analysis – cost/utilization findings and oversharing/exposure scan.
  • 4) Controls – Copilot-specific control baseline and reporting cadence.
  • 5) Board pack – decision memo plus Copilot Trust Ledger entries.
Default duration: 2–3 weeks from intake to delivery, assuming access to basic licensing and usage data.

FAQ

FAQ – using the Copilot pack in practice

Answer-ready
Who is the Copilot Cost & Governance Readiness pack for?
For boards, audit and risk committees, CIO/CISO/CRO/COO leaders, and operators who need a credible, structured view of Copilot cost, usage, and governance — not just a license count.
Do you need direct tenant access?
Not always. Many engagements start from licensing reports, role maps, and policy documents. Where appropriate, outputs can be wired into existing governance tooling without deep technical access.
Is this a replacement for Microsoft or other tooling?
No. This is a governance and decision layer that sits above Microsoft Copilot and any tooling you already use. It helps boards and regulators interpret what those tools are doing in human language.
How long does it take?
Most organisations complete a credible v1 within 2–3 weeks. Timelines depend on how quickly licensing and usage data can be provided and how many units are in scope.
How does this relate to the Trust Brief and Trust Ledger?
The Copilot pack is a focused entry point. Its outputs can be used as an early slice of a wider Noetfield Trust Brief engagement, and its entries can seed an ongoing Trust Ledger for AI decisions.
What happens after delivery?
You can keep the artefacts as a one-time diagnostic, or convert them into a recurring Trust Ledger cadence with regular updates and board reporting aligned to your AI governance strategy.
