Why this exists

Copilot is not only a license line item. It expands the governance surface: new access patterns, new agents, new content flows, and new failure modes. Cost and risk drift unless recorded and governed.

  • Clarify Copilot scope (which experiences are in use, by whom, and for what)
  • Detect license waste and adoption gaps
  • Inventory Copilot agents and ownership
  • Reduce oversharing and orphaned / outdated sources
  • Produce board-ready decision artefacts

Output in one sentence

A short, auditable “Copilot decision pack” plus Trust Ledger entries that a board, audit committee, or regulator can read.

What you get

1) Copilot cost map

Clarifies Copilot types in use, licensing context, and where spend can drift.

2) License utilization report

Assigned vs active usage, waste hotspots, adoption blockers, and a cleanup plan.

3) Agent inventory (MVP Ledger)

Who created what, ownership, purpose, data scope, and minimum controls.

4) Oversharing and exposure scan

Risk paths for sensitive content and common misconfigurations (with remediation steps).

5) Control baseline

Policies, lifecycle rules, reporting cadence, and accountability assignments.

6) Board decision memo

Proceed / pause / limited pilot / scale, with explicit conditions and a 90-day plan.

How it works

  1. Intake (48h): current licensing context, target groups, and governance constraints.
  2. Mapping: Copilot scope + agent inventory + high-risk content paths.
  3. Recording: draft Trust Ledger entries and cost/utilization findings.
  4. Controls: baseline policies, lifecycle rules, and reporting cadence.
  5. Board pack: decision memo + 90-day operating plan.
