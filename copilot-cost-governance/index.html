At a glance

What the Copilot cost & governance pack includes

The Copilot pack gives you a short, auditable snapshot of how Copilot is deployed, what it costs, where it drifts, and which controls exist – without a long consulting project or a full tool rollout.

  • Clear map of Copilot scope: who is using what, where, and for which types of work.
  • License utilization and waste: assigned vs active use, adoption gaps, cleanup opportunities.
  • Agent inventory: who created which agents, for what purpose, with which data access.
  • Exposure view: high-risk content flows and oversharing patterns to address first.
  • Baseline controls: simple policies, lifecycle rules, and accountability assignments.

Why this exists

Copilot is not just a license line item

Copilot changes the governance surface of your organisation: new access patterns, new agents, and new routes for sensitive content. Without a clear record, cost and risk drift quietly in the background.

The problems this pack targets
No single place that shows where Copilot is enabled and why.
Licenses assigned but barely used – or used for the wrong work.
Agents created and abandoned with unclear ownership or scope.
Oversharing of documents, sites, or channels “just to make it work”.
Boards and auditors asking questions without a concise answer set.

The goal is not to replace Microsoft or any governance tool vendor. The goal is to give you a board-ready record: a short decision memo and a small set of Trust Ledger entries that can sit above whatever tools you already use.

That record can then be reused in board packs, risk reviews, external audits, or regulatory conversations – without re-doing the analysis each time.

Outputs

What you receive at the end of the engagement

1) Copilot cost map

A concise view of Copilot license types, target groups, and potential drift areas – including where spend is likely to grow or shrink.

2) License utilization report

Assigned vs active usage, obvious waste, adoption blockers, and a simple cleanup and reallocation plan aligned with your governance posture.

3) Agent inventory (MVP ledger)

An initial list of key Copilot agents: who owns them, what they are for, which data they touch, and what minimum controls they should observe.

4) Exposure & oversharing scan

Practical view of high-risk content paths – such as overly broad sharing, orphaned sites, or outdated sources – with priority remediation steps.

5) Control baseline

Simple, implementable rules: where Copilot is allowed, who can create agents, how they are reviewed, and what reporting cadence is expected.

6) Board decision memo & Trust Ledger entries

A 3–5 page decision memo and 1–3 Trust Ledger entries that capture today’s Copilot posture and a 90-day plan to proceed, pause, or scale with conditions.

Process

How the Copilot pack works – async-first by design

The engagement is time-boxed and light on meetings. Most work is driven by documents, exports, and written questions – so your team does not need to add a new recurring call.

  1. Step 1 – Short intake (2–3 days)
    You complete a brief form and, if available, share existing license reports, Copilot settings, and any policy notes. Scope and fee are confirmed in a 1–2 page written note.
  2. Step 2 – Mapping & analysis (approx. 1 week)
    Noetfield reviews the material, prepares a first map of Copilot scope, license utilization, agent inventory, and likely exposure paths. Clarifications are handled via email or short targeted questions.
  3. Step 3 – Draft decision pack & Trust Ledger entries
    A draft decision memo and Trust Ledger entries are prepared and shared for written feedback from 1–3 key stakeholders.
  4. Step 4 – Final pack & 90-day plan
    You receive the final pack in PDF plus a simple table (CSV or spreadsheet) that can be reused internally. If desired, a short remote briefing can be added, but it is optional.

FAQ

Common questions about the Copilot pack

Do you need direct tenant or admin access?
Not always. The pack can start from exports, policy documents, and role-based descriptions of how Copilot is used. Where technical telemetry exists, it can be incorporated without changing your existing tool stack.
Is this a replacement for Microsoft or a governance tool vendor?
No. The Copilot pack is a governance and decision layer that sits above your existing tools. It is designed to be read by boards, auditors, and regulators – and to complement, not replace, any tools you already use.
Can this be delivered entirely remotely?
Yes. The default model is fully remote and async-first, using email, documents, and written questions. If a live briefing is helpful at the end, it can be scheduled separately.
What happens after the pack is delivered?
You can keep using the pack as a static record, or convert the Trust Ledger entries into a small ongoing Noetfield Trust Ledger engagement, with a light reporting cadence for key AI systems.
Can we extend the scope beyond Copilot?
Yes. For organisations with multiple AI tools, the Copilot pack can be the first module of a broader AI Trust Brief or Trust Ledger rollout. Any extensions are scoped and priced separately so you retain control.
What if we are early and not yet live with Copilot?
The same structure can be used as a pre-deployment readiness review, focused on scope, controls, and decision criteria before licenses are fully rolled out.
