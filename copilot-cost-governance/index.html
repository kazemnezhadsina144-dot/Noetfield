Definition

What the Copilot pack is

Practical & governance-first

The Noetfield Copilot Cost & Governance Readiness pack is a compact, time-boxed engagement that creates a clear record of how Copilot is deployed, what it costs, where it drifts, and which controls exist – without standing up a full programme or new tooling layer.

It assumes Copilot is either already live or about to be rolled out to meaningful parts of the organisation. Instead of adding more dashboards, the pack focuses on what boards, risk committees, and auditors need: a small set of artefacts that summarise scope, cost, exposure, and decisions in plain language.

The language and structure are compatible with Noetfield’s Trust Brief and Trust Ledger vocabulary, so the Copilot work can be extended into broader AI governance without starting from zero.

In one sentence: a short Copilot-focused engagement that delivers a board-readable cost and governance record, plus a 90-day plan.
Inside the pack
Core components at a glance

Each module produces concrete artefacts that can plug into existing governance routines.

  • Copilot scope & mandate — which experiences, for whom, and for what types of work.
  • License view — how licenses are assigned, used, and where waste appears.
  • Agent & use-case inventory — key agents, owners, data, and review points.
  • Exposure paths — oversharing, high-risk content routes, and misconfigurations.
  • Control baseline — simple rules, approvals, and reporting cadence.
  • Decision memo — proceed / pause / limited roll-out / scale with conditions.
  • Trust Ledger entries — 1–3 entries capturing today’s posture and plan.

What you get

Concrete outputs after 2–3 weeks

The pack is designed to finish with artefacts you can attach to board packs, risk reviews, and audits.

  • A single Copilot cost map that explains license structure and expected drift.
  • A short utilization and waste report with concrete cleanup and reallocation steps.
  • An inventory of key Copilot agents and use-cases with named owners and data scope.
  • An exposure and oversharing view, focused on practical, high-impact remediation.
  • A 3–5 page decision memo with a 90-day plan to proceed, pause, or scale with conditions.
  • 1–3 Trust Ledger entries that record today’s Copilot posture in a repeatable format.

Best suited for organisations where:

  • Licenses have been purchased, but governance and cost narratives lag behind.

Typical roles involved:

  • Board, audit, and risk committee chairs.

FAQ

FAQ – using the Copilot pack in practice

Answer-ready
Who is the Copilot Cost & Governance Readiness pack for?
For organisations where Copilot is either live or about to be rolled out at meaningful scale, and where boards, auditors, or internal risk teams are starting to ask for a clear picture of cost, usage, and controls.
Does this replace Microsoft or governance tools?
No. It sits above your existing tools as a governance and decision layer. It produces artefacts that can reference Microsoft telemetry, policies, and existing controls rather than duplicating them.
How much internal time does it take?
Most of the work is document- and export-driven. Typical pattern: one main contact, a small number of short clarification exchanges, and a review of the draft decision memo and Ledger entries before finalisation.
Can this be extended into broader AI governance?
Yes. The Copilot pack is often a first step towards a broader Trust Brief or Trust Ledger engagement. The same structures and language can be reused for other AI systems and high-impact use-cases.
Can this support upcoming audits or board meetings?
Yes. The engagement is time-boxed to align with concrete events such as board cycles, audit windows, or regulatory milestones, as long as those dates are clear at the outset.
Is everything delivered remotely?
Yes. The default model is fully remote and async-first. If a live briefing is helpful once the pack is complete, it can be added as an optional session.
