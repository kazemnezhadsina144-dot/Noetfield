3) What you get (QuickScan deliverables)

Procurement-ready, not slide-heavy

Deliverables are designed to be filed, forwarded, and actioned.

  • Scope lock: Copilot rollout boundary (who/where/what) with explicit assumptions.
  • Oversharing exposure map: high-risk data surfaces (SharePoint, Teams, OneDrive patterns) and priority hotspots.
  • Sensitive-data boundaries: what must not be exposed + guardrails for prompts and access behavior.
  • Connector / extension control notes: what to allow, block, or review first.
  • Evidence pack: what exists today vs what is missing for audit/procurement confidence.
  • 30/60/90 plan: owners, sequencing, and decision points (proceed / pause / remediate).
Measured on outcomes: confirmed risk reduction actions + decision readiness, not document volume.
4) Timeline

Fast by design

QuickScan is built to compress uncertainty quickly.

  • Day 0: non-confidential intake → scope lock + access readiness plan.
  • Days 1–4: evidence collection + stakeholder alignment checkpoints (minimal meetings).
  • Days 5–7: deliverables packaged + decision options + next-step plan.
What slows it down: unclear scope, unclear owners, and no access plan. QuickScan exists to fix those first.

7) FAQ

Common buyer questions

  • Is the first intake confidential? No. Public intake is non-confidential by design. A secure channel follows if there is a fit.

  • Is this a technical implementation service? QuickScan is decision + evidence first. Remediation Sprint handles execution if requested.
  • Can procurement use the outputs? Yes — outputs are designed to be forwarded, filed, and referenced in procurement workflows.
