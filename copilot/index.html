Definition

What QuickScan is

Fixed scope

QuickScan is a time-boxed, evidence-first Copilot governance assessment. It focuses on what boards, auditors, and clients ask for: enablement scope, defensible boundaries, exportable evidence, and clear controls around extensions and custom copilots.

Core rule: if a control cannot be supported by evidence (config, export, log), it cannot be claimed.

Deliverables

What the client receives

Board pack ready

Delivered as a concise executive summary plus evidence-first artefacts that can be reused internally.

  • D0 — Executive Summary (1 page, RAG): overall status + 5-pillar RAG + decision requests.
  • D1 — Copilot Surface Inventory & Enablement Map.
  • D2 — Oversharing Heatmap (Top 10): ranked actions (quick wins vs projects).
  • D3 — Data Boundaries Pack + Red-Line Rules v1: publish-ready internal memo.
  • D4 — Evidence Readiness Sheet: signals, retention posture, export steps, owners.
  • D5 — Extension/Connector Control Register: allowed/restricted tools + change control + exception protocol.
  • 30/60/90 plan: quick wins + governance milestones and owners.
Routing logic: QuickScan often precedes a broader Trust Brief and, where needed, ongoing Trust Ledger operations.
Process

Process and timing

5–7 business days
  • Day 0–1: scope lock + access readiness.
  • Day 2–4: inventory + posture review + evidence capture.
  • Day 5–7: synthesis + delivery of board-ready pack.
Access

Access required

Minimal by default

Read-only administrative access is preferred where possible, sufficient to export configuration and reporting signals across in-scope Microsoft 365 workloads. No content sampling unless explicitly authorized.

Required client roles

  • M365 Admin
  • Security / Compliance lead
  • Business owner (Copilot adoption owner)
Common triggers
Board or audit questions are starting to appear.
Copilot is scaling beyond a small pilot.
External sharing is non-trivial.
High-sensitivity domains exist (HR/finance/legal/client).
Limitations

What QuickScan does not do

Clear boundaries
  • Remediation implementation (available as a separate scope).
  • Content-level review or sampling (only with explicit authorization).
  • Adversarial testing (separate scope).
Add-ons

Optional extensions

Modular
  • Remediation Sprint (2–3 weeks) + re-scan
  • Copilot Studio deep governance program
  • Quarterly evidence refresh (delta report)
  • Connector/vendor egress review

FAQ

FAQ

Answer-ready
FAQ
Who is this for?

Boards, audit and risk committees, and technology or risk leaders enabling Copilot at scale or already in production and needing defensible boundaries and exportable evidence.

FAQ
Does this replace Microsoft tools?

No. QuickScan sits above existing tools as a governance and decision layer focused on board- and audit-readable artefacts.

FAQ
Is this implementation?

No. Implementation and remediation can be scoped as an add-on after the evidence pack is delivered.

FAQ
Does this require admin access?

Read-only access is preferred. Where not available, the engagement can begin from exports and documented configurations.
