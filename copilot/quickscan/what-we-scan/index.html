Five pillars

Each pillar maps to a named output in the Buyer Pack.

1) Copilot surface inventory + enablement map

Scope clarity: where Copilot exists and who it is enabled for.

  • Enabled Copilot experiences across M365 surfaces (and Copilot Studio/custom copilots if present).
  • Audience scope and tenant posture (high-level, configuration-oriented).
2) Permission sprawl + oversharing exposure paths

Exposure visibility driven by existing permissions and sharing posture.

  • High-risk sharing patterns (broad groups, inherited permissions, “everyone” style access).
  • Where Copilot could amplify unintended access based on existing permissions.
3) Data boundaries + sensitive content controls

Boundary definition for high-risk domains and internal rules.

  • High-risk domains aligned (HR / finance / legal / client data; context-dependent).
  • DLP/label posture snapshot and boundary rule draft (“red-lines”).
4) Logging, retention, and evidence readiness

What counts as proof, and how to export it.

  • Signals available (usage/admin changes/key events) and retention posture.
  • Export steps and named owners for evidence production.
5) Extension/connector/custom Copilot controls

Controls for tool access and egress surfaces (if present).

  • Allowed vs restricted connectors/extensions posture.
  • Change control, exceptions, and break-glass rules for high-risk actions.
