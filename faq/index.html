About

What Noetfield is (and isn’t).

What is Noetfield?
Noetfield is an evidence-first AI governance practice. Delivery focuses on converting scattered AI artefacts into board-ready and procurement-ready records: Trust Ledger entries and a decision-grade Trust Brief.
Who is this for?
Teams where AI affects material decisions (money, safety, access, compliance, operations). Common stakeholders: executives, operators, security, privacy, legal, procurement, and boards.
Is Noetfield a SaaS tool?
Not today. Current delivery is artefacts + evidence discipline (reports, ledgers, control mappings, acceptance criteria). Portal-style workflows may be introduced later, but you can route and track via Gate and Status.
What Noetfield is not
Noetfield is not a general AI dev shop, not a chatbot agency, and not a marketing automation vendor. Delivery stays governance-first and partner-friendly: controls, evidence, decision records.

Copilot readiness

Microsoft 365 Copilot governance, controls, and rollouts.

What is a Copilot Readiness Assessment?
A Copilot readiness assessment is a structured review of data boundaries, permissions, DLP, audit/retention, and operational controls required for safe rollout. Output is a board/procurement-ready pack, not a slide deck. See Copilot Readiness.
What is Copilot QuickScan?
Copilot QuickScan is a rapid readiness snapshot (typically 5–7 business days) that identifies the highest-risk exposure themes and the fastest control moves (permissions, sharing, DLP, audit, retention, connector posture).
What does “data boundaries” mean for Copilot?
“Data boundaries” means ensuring Copilot cannot surface sensitive content to the wrong users. It typically includes SharePoint/OneDrive/Teams permission hygiene, external sharing posture, DLP rules, label usage, and auditability of access and changes.
Do connectors and extensions change the risk profile?
Yes. Connectors and extensions expand the data surface area. Readiness should confirm connector allowlists, admin controls, logging, and least-privilege access patterns before enabling broad access.

Trust Brief & Trust Ledger

What you get and how it works.

What’s the difference between Trust Brief and Trust Ledger?
Trust Brief is a time-boxed diagnostic and decision memo (commonly 6 weeks): what matters, what risks exist, what controls are missing, and what options the board can approve.

Trust Ledger is the durable record: ledger entries with control statements and proofs (evidence) that remain readable over time.
What do I receive in a Trust Brief?
Typical outputs (scope varies by organization):
  • Material AI use-case map (what AI is doing, where, and why it matters)
  • Control baseline and gaps (governance, security, privacy, operations)
  • Evidence plan (what proofs are needed, and where they come from)
  • Executive decision memo (approve / pause / remediate / pilot)
  • Initial Trust Ledger entries for the highest-risk items
Start at Trust Brief.
How long does it take?
Trust Brief is designed around a 6-week cadence. Trust Ledger is ongoing and can be built in phases. For urgent triage, route via Gate and include your deadline.
Do you need confidential access or data uploads?
No. Gate forms are non-confidential. Use sanitized descriptions or public links only. If deeper access becomes necessary, it happens later under an explicit scope and agreement (not through public intake).

Delivery model

How work is executed and accepted.

How does delivery work?
A typical engagement is:
  • Kickoff: scope, stakeholders, constraints, acceptance criteria
  • Evidence capture plan: what will be proven, and how
  • Build: ledger entries, control mapping, decision memo draft
  • Review: stakeholder review and corrections
  • Delivery: board/procurement-ready pack + handoff
What counts as “done”?
“Done” is defined by acceptance criteria: the minimum evidence quality, required artefacts, and the decision outputs expected (e.g., board memo, control mapping, ledger entries, procurement FAQ). This is agreed at kickoff.

Evidence & security

Why “proofs” matter and how risk is reduced.

What counts as evidence (“proof”)?
Evidence is anything that survives scrutiny: configuration exports, control settings, audit logs, policy objects, ticket history, change records, and repeatable SOPs. The point is defensibility, not slides.
Why do you prohibit confidential uploads in public forms?
Public intake is for routing, not data transfer. This reduces risk and prevents accidental exposure. Sensitive sharing must happen later under explicit scope, access controls, and an agreed channel.
Do you sign NDAs?
If needed, NDAs are handled after initial fit is confirmed via Gate. Start with a non-confidential one-paragraph scope.

Procurement & pricing

How pricing is set and how buyers approve work.

What does it cost?
Pricing depends on scope, complexity, urgency, and procurement requirements. Typical anchors:
  • Copilot Readiness Assessment: Starter (2 weeks) 9,500 CAD · Standard (4 weeks) 15,000 CAD · Extended (6 weeks) 25,000 CAD
  • Trust Brief: typically a low five-figure range depending on surface area
  • Trust Ledger: phased build or retainer, based on number of material AI use-cases and evidence surfaces
For an exact quote, submit one paragraph via Gate (scope + deadline + procurement path).
How do payments work?
Payment method follows the buyer’s procurement route (card, invoice, PO, net terms). If procurement needs a specific format, note it in Gate or start at /gate/sales/.
Do you offer refunds?
Refund and cancellation terms depend on the agreed scope and phase. Baseline terms are published at /terms/.
Procurement note: If a buyer requires DPA/NDA, data retention, or security posture answers, include it in Gate. Public intake remains non-confidential.

Gate, Request ID, Status

Routing and tracking without email loops.

What is Gate?
Gate is the controlled entry point for requests (clients, partners, offers). It assigns a Request ID so routing and follow-up are deterministic.
What is a Request ID and why does it matter?
Request ID (RID) is your tracking key across Gate, Status, and Thanks pages. It reduces ambiguity and keeps evidence trails connected to the right intake.
How do I check status?
Open /status/ and include your RID (often auto-appended like ?rid=...). If you lost it, check your browser history or any “Thanks” redirect URL.
I submitted a form but didn’t see the “Thanks” page — did it work?
Possible causes: network interruption, blocked scripts, or endpoint error. Re-open and submit again if needed. If you have the RID, use Feedback and include it.
Fast path: If routing is needed now, use Gate and include a deadline + one-paragraph scope.

Partners

Delivery, integration, and channel lanes.

Who should apply as a partner?
Partners are teams who can ship repeatable outcomes with evidence discipline: governance delivery packs, integration/security controls, or channel distribution. Start at Partners Gate.
What is the Delivery lane?
Delivery lane partners can deliver Trust Brief/Ledger artefacts as a clean, repeatable package (SOPs, templates, acceptance criteria, and evidence capture).
What is the Integration / Security lane?
This lane is for teams who can wire up identity, audit logs, DLP, and controls so governance artefacts have a defensible evidence trail.
Can I submit confidential proofs or client documents?
Not via public forms. Use sanitized descriptions or public links only. If deeper verification is required, it is handled later under explicit scope and controls.

Other

Common operational clarifications.

Do you work async or only by meetings?
Delivery is optimized for async progress with structured checkpoints. Meetings are used for kickoff and decision reviews, not for constant back-and-forth.
What if we are not ready yet?
That is a valid outcome. The work can end with a pause/remediate recommendation and a prioritized control plan, rather than forcing rollout. The goal is decision clarity and defensible posture.
