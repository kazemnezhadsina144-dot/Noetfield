About

What Noetfield is (and isn’t).

What is Noetfield?
Noetfield is an evidence-first AI governance practice. We turn scattered AI artefacts into board-ready decision records: a durable Trust Ledger and an executive-grade Trust Brief.
Who is this for?
Teams where AI affects material decisions (money, safety, access, compliance, operations). Common buyers: executives, operators, risk, legal, security, and boards. See For Whom.
Is Noetfield a SaaS tool?
Not today. Current delivery is governance artefacts + evidence patterns. “Platform/Portal” areas are being staged; you can still route through Gate.

Trust Brief & Trust Ledger

What you get and how it works.

What’s the difference between Trust Brief and Trust Ledger?
Trust Brief is the time-boxed diagnostic and decision memo (commonly 6 weeks): what matters, what risks exist, what controls are missing, and what options the board can approve.

Trust Ledger is the durable record: ledger entries with controls + proofs (evidence) that remain readable over time.
What do I receive in a Trust Brief?
Typical outputs (scope varies by organization):
  • Material AI use-case map (what AI is doing, where, and why it matters)
  • Control baseline and gaps (governance, security, privacy, operations)
  • Evidence plan (what proofs are needed, and where they come from)
  • Board-ready options memo (approve / pause / remediate / pilot)
  • Initial Trust Ledger entries for the highest-risk items
Start at Trust Brief.
How long does it take?
Trust Brief is designed around a 6-week cadence. Trust Ledger is ongoing and can be built in phases. If you need urgent triage, route via Gate and include your deadline.
Do you need confidential access or data uploads?
No. Gate forms are non-confidential. Use links or sanitized descriptions. If deeper access is needed later, it happens under a proper scope and agreement (not in public forms).

Gate, Request ID, Status

Routing and tracking without email loops.

What is Gate?
Gate is the controlled entry point for requests (clients, partners, offers). It assigns a Request ID so routing and follow-up is deterministic.
What is a Request ID and why does it matter?
Request ID (RID) is your tracking key across Gate, Status, and Thanks pages. It reduces ambiguity and keeps evidence trails connected to the right intake.
How do I check status?
Open /status/ and include your RID (often auto-appended like ?rid=...). If you lost it, check your browser history or any “Thanks” redirect URL.
I submitted a form but didn’t see the “Thanks” page — did it work?
Possible causes: network interruption, blocked scripts, or endpoint error. Re-open the page and submit again if needed. If you have the RID, use Feedback and include it.
Fast path: If you need routing now, use Gate and include a deadline + scope in one paragraph.
Non-confidential rule: No credentials, internal security details, or private documents in any public form.

Partners

Delivery, integration, and channel lanes.

Who should apply as a partner?
Partners are teams who can ship repeatable outcomes with evidence discipline: integration/security controls, governance delivery packs, or channel distribution. Start at Partners.
What is the Delivery lane?
Delivery lane partners can deliver Trust Brief/Ledger artefacts as a clean, repeatable package (with SOPs, templates, acceptance criteria, and evidence capture).
What is the Integration / Security lane?
This lane is for teams who can wire up identity, audit logs, DLP, and controls so governance artefacts have a defensible evidence trail.
Can I submit confidential proofs or client documents?
Not via public forms. Use sanitized/public links only. If deeper verification is needed, it’s handled later under proper scope.

Security & Evidence

Why “proofs” matter.

What counts as evidence (“proof”)?
Evidence is anything that survives scrutiny: configuration exports, control settings, audit logs, policy objects, ticket history, change records, and repeatable SOPs. The point is defensibility, not slides.
Why do you prohibit confidential uploads in forms?
Public intake is for routing, not data transfer. This reduces risk and prevents accidental exposure. Sensitive sharing must happen later under explicit scope and controls.
Do you sign NDAs?
If needed, NDAs are handled after initial fit is confirmed via Gate. Start with a non-confidential one-paragraph scope through Gate.
