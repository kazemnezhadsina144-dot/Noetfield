Where Noetfield is most relevant

Real-economy sectors

Noetfield focuses on organisations where AI decisions have real external consequences – for customers, citizens, or markets. Typical partners include:

  • Banks, credit unions, and other financial institutions.
  • Infrastructure and utilities (energy, transport, communications).
  • Insurance, pensions, and asset management.
  • Critical public services and delegated authorities.

In these environments, AI decisions can change prices, access, and outcomes – and governance must keep pace with technical capability.

Signals you are in the right place
AI or automation already influences credit, pricing, onboarding, or trading.
Regulators are starting to ask “how” decisions are taken, not just “if” AI is used.
Board packs mention AI, but there is no stable way to record decisions and safeguards.
Documentation exists, but is written for engineers, not directors or supervisors.
You have experienced an incident or near-miss where AI played a role.

Use-case snapshots

Examples of where a Trust Brief and Trust Ledger make a difference.

Financial services
Retail credit and limits

AI-assisted credit decisioning is already in production. The board wants assurance that limit changes, overrides, and fairness safeguards are governed – not just monitored. A Trust Brief produces Ledger entries for key credit decisions and shows where committees were in the loop.

Infrastructure

Who Noetfield is not for

The Trust Brief and Trust Ledger are not designed for every AI context. In particular, Noetfield is usually not the right fit if:

  • The primary goal is to build or tune models, not to govern decisions.
  • AI is used only in non-material, internal productivity tools.
  • The focus is short-term experimentation without accountability to customers or supervisors.
  • There is no board- or committee-level interest in AI governance.

In those cases, classic MLOps, experimentation platforms, or general AI consulting may be more appropriate.

What makes a good partner

  • A clear recognition that AI has moved into core, material processes.
  • Executive willingness to expose and improve current governance, not just showcase successes.
  • Board or committee interest in having a stable artefact for AI decisions.
  • A small cross-functional group willing to work together over six weeks.

FAQ – fit and expectations

Do we need a mature AI programme to work with Noetfield?
No. Many partners start with pockets of AI and automation embedded in existing systems or vendor tools. What matters is that some of these already influence material decisions. The Trust Brief helps make that landscape visible and governable.
Is Noetfield an AI vendor or a fund?
Noetfield is neither a model vendor nor an investment fund. It is a neutral governance layer focused on how AI decisions are documented, surfaced, and owned. We work alongside your existing vendors, tools, and advisors.
Can internal teams maintain the Trust Ledger after a project?
Yes. One of the goals of the Trust Brief is to establish clear ownership and a maintainable pattern for Ledger entries. After the initial work, many organisations continue to extend and update the Ledger themselves as systems and regulations evolve.
How does this relate to our existing risk and control frameworks?
The Trust Ledger and Trust Brief are designed to sit on top of existing frameworks – model risk management, operational risk, conduct, or technology risk. We map Ledger entries to those structures so that AI governance is integrated, not separate.
