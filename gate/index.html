Why this matters now

Boards are being asked to sign off on AI systems they cannot see. Regulators are tightening expectations. Logs exist everywhere – but accountability is nowhere in one place.

  • AI regulation and fiduciary duties are converging – boards must show how they governed critical AI decisions, not just that systems were deployed.
  • Technical logs, tickets, and dashboards are unreadable at board level, and often scattered across multiple systems.
  • Without a short, auditable history of key AI decisions, organisations risk unanswerable questions after an incident, investigation, or audit.

The Trust Ledger: a short, auditable history of your most critical AI decisions

Technical teams keep detailed logs. The Trust Ledger is something different: a disciplined record of a few critical decisions in plain language that can sit in front of the board, auditors, and regulators.

Decisions
What really matters

Which AI systems, models, and use cases are material – and when a change in behaviour or scope becomes a board-level decision.

Data & safeguards
How they are controlled

What data these systems rely on, how they are controlled, and which safeguards, limits, and monitoring are in place in practice – not just on paper.

Accountability
Who decided what, when

Who decided what, when, and based on which evidence – and how that decision was surfaced to the board, risk committee, or oversight body.

About the thesis

Noetfield is being built as a neutral governance layer – not a fund, not an AI vendor – so boards, executives, and regulators can anchor trust in one place.

The work sits between the mutual model of stewardship (long-horizon asset owners) and the public-market scale of major managers. The Trust Ledger is the first wedge: a small, concrete artefact that can scale into a broader system.

Background spans law, governance, and AI-adjacent systems. The focus is simple: give serious institutions a way to govern AI that matches both regulatory pressure and moral responsibility – without drowning them in more noise.
