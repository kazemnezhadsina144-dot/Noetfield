What this pilot solves (in plain language)
Decision-grade • audit-friendly
The problem

AI initiatives produce “artefacts” (policies, dashboards, validations, minutes, vendor docs) but boards and supervisors need a single, defensible view of where AI is material and how it is governed.

The outcome
  • Trust Brief (typical 12–16 pages): the board/committee-ready view of material AI decisions, safeguards, and gaps.
  • Trust Ledger slice: structured entries for the highest-signal decision paths (thin by design, refreshable).
  • MAP (Management Action Plan): 3–8 governance moves with owners, due dates, and evidence expectations.
Typical inputs (non-invasive)
  • 6–10 stakeholder interviews (risk, security, privacy, business owners, IT/M365 owners).
  • 10–25 existing artefacts reviewed (policies, controls, logs/exports, minutes, vendor docs).
  • 2–4 short workshops (materiality, boundary mapping, governance packaging).

Noetfield prioritizes reuse of existing artefacts over net-new documentation.

Pilot structure

6 weeks • three passes • minimal disruption
Time-boxed
Weeks 1–2
Scope + artefact sweep
Confirm decision paths, collect existing evidence, define boundaries and committee questions.
Weeks 3–4
Mapping + draft Brief
Ledger schema mapping, draft Trust Brief, interim review with sponsor group.
Weeks 5–6
Finalize + governance packaging
Finalize Brief and Ledger slice, produce MAP and board-ready decision options.
Board-ready artefacts (deliverable set)
  • Trust Brief (PDF): exec summary, material decisions, safeguards, gaps, options.
  • Ledger slice (PDF/online): thin set of entries for material decision paths.
  • Evidence Snapshot: what exists, what’s missing, and what is “current.”
  • Control Taxonomy: mapping from policy/control areas to the institution’s governance language.
  • Roadmap 30/60/90: concrete next steps post-pilot.

What changes (signals)

The board conversation shifts from “where is AI?” to “how is this governed?”
Reusable
Governance signals (illustrative)
Board & committees
  • Board Risk Committee receives a concise, repeatable view of material AI decision paths.
  • Committees adopt a small set of recurring AI questions anchored to the Ledger (cadence-ready).
  • Risk appetite discussion becomes decision-specific, not theme-based.
Executives & supervisors
  • Executives gain a single artefact that supports supervisor, investor, or funder discussions.
  • Teams map new AI initiatives to the Ledger rather than restarting from zero.
  • Supervisory conversations become governance-based: controls, boundaries, oversight.
When this pattern fits
  • You have multiple AI initiatives but no single board-ready view of what is genuinely material.
  • You expect more pointed supervisory or audit questions within the next 12–18 months.
  • You want to build on what already exists (artefacts, logs, policies) rather than deploy a new platform just to describe governance.
  • You prefer a time-boxed pilot with concrete outputs and a clear “what next” path.
