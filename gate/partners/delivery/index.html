1) What partners deliver (minimum)

A “good deal” starts with discipline. Minimum bar for any partner-driven intro:

  • Buyer fit: AI is used in material decisions (ops, finance, safety, compliance, HR, procurement).
  • Sponsor: an exec sponsor exists (or can be introduced within 7 days).
  • Scope: 1–3 material AI use cases (named, not vague).
  • Timeline: decision window stated (even if “unknown”, state why).
  • Budget posture: clear willingness to pay for governance artefacts (not “free consulting”).
  • Evidence discipline: buyer accepts documentation and auditability as part of the work.
2) Handoff package (partner → Noetfield)

Send these as a single message or doc. No confidential documents in forms.

  • RID: include the RID in subject line and first sentence.
  • Intro note: why this buyer, why now (2–6 sentences).
  • Buyer roles: names optional; roles required (COO/CFO/CIO/Compliance Lead, etc.).
  • Use case sketch: what the AI does, where it touches material outcomes.
  • Risks: any known compliance/regulatory/board sensitivities.
  • Next action: propose a single next step (discovery call, memo review, pilot proposal).
