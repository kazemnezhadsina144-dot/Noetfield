Overview

What Noetfield is building

Trust Ledger for AI

Noetfield is a governance-first Trust Ledger layer for AI. The platform helps institutions answer four simple questions:

  • Where is AI used in our organization?
  • What data, models, and vendors are involved?
  • What risks, safeguards, and accountabilities exist – and who owns them?
  • What can we show our board and regulators today?

Noetfield combines a 6-week Trust Brief – a focused AI governance diagnostic – with an annual Trust Ledger workspace that keeps this view live across time, frameworks, and incidents.

In one sentence: Noetfield gives boards and regulators a plain-language, auditable Trust Ledger for AI – not another model, but the governance layer on top of existing models and workflows.
At a glance
Trust Brief & Trust Ledger

Trust Brief (6 weeks). Maps 1–3 material AI use cases, assesses risks and controls, and delivers a board-ready Trust Brief with initial Trust Ledger entries and a 90-day roadmap.

Trust Ledger (annual). A living registry and reporting workspace for AI use, data assets, dependencies, safeguards, and board-level decisions.

For investors

Investment thesis & capital track

Evidence-first

Noetfield is not a general AI agency and not a commodity SaaS tool. It is a governance and Trust Ledger layer that helps boards and regulators see, audit, and steer AI in real-economy institutions.

The commercial model is simple:

  • Trust Brief (T1) – 6-week diagnostic; board-ready AI governance dossier; main entry and proof-of-value engine.
  • Trust Ledger Core (T2) – annual governance baseline (~18K CAD / year).
  • Trust Ledger Plus (T3) – compliance & integration layer (~45K CAD / year).

Over an 18–24 month horizon, a focused portfolio of ~30 Core and ~10 Plus customers, with 15–20 Trust Briefs per year feeding the pipeline, anchors a path to ~1M CAD in recurring governance revenue. A 6× ARR multiple – typical for governance/compliance infrastructure once early product–market fit is established – underpins a ~6M CAD valuation band.

At this stage, we are open to small, aligned cheques that buy time and focus to deliver the first wave of Trust Brief pilots, and to investors who are comfortable with an evidence-first path: real contracts, board-facing artifacts, and a concentrated portfolio in 1–2 verticals rather than hypergrowth on paper.

Why now

Why AI governance needs a Trust Ledger

  • AI is already embedded in credit, fraud detection, trading, grid operations, and customer service – but governance is still ad-hoc.
  • Boards and regulators are being asked to sign off on AI risk without a single, auditable ledger of decisions and safeguards.
  • Existing GRC tools were not built for model drift, bias, explainability, and third-party model dependencies.
  • A neutral, board-readable Trust Ledger is becoming a necessity, not a luxury.
