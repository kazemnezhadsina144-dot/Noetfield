Definition

What this partners track is

High-risk · non-public

This page is not a fundraising campaign and not a retail product. It is a controlled, invitation-only track for a small number of partners who want to support the early build-out of a governance layer that boards and regulators can actually use.

The partners track typically supports three early necessities:

  • Pilots. 1–3 Trust Brief pilots producing board-ready artifacts and initial Trust Ledger entries.
  • Productization. Turning the pilot pattern into a repeatable, sellable operating system.
  • Governance infrastructure. Legal, policy, security, and audit posture to remain institutional-grade.
Principle: capital discipline should match governance discipline — clear structure, clean boundaries, and honest risk disclosure.

Process

What happens next

Asynchronous-first

The partners track is run as a controlled process. If the profile is aligned, the next step is a private exchange of context (asynchronous-first), followed by optional documentation review.

  • Step 1. Submit interest (below) and receive a Request ID for tracking.
  • Step 2. Fit screening (mandate, risk posture, eligibility, and partner path).
  • Step 3. If aligned: NDA + a small, structured packet (structure + notes + diligence checklist).
  • Step 4. Draft documentation (where applicable) and staged next actions.
Service posture: clarity and pace over pressure — no public solicitation, no retail funnel tactics.
Hard compliance boundaries
No public offering language. No promises, forecasts, or “guaranteed returns.”

FAQ

Answer-ready FAQ

Board-safe
Is this a public offering or crowdfunding page?
No. This is a private partners track. Nothing on this page is an offer to sell or a solicitation to buy securities. Any participation would only proceed via applicable exemptions and final executed documentation.
Why run partners through a capital spine instead of direct common equity?
Early-stage capitalization often benefits from clean operating structures. A capital spine can consolidate early commitments, maintain cleaner cap tables for future institutional rounds, and keep governance and risk disclosures disciplined.
What is the core wedge you are building?
A board-first Trust Brief pilot pattern that produces defensible artifacts and seeds a Trust Ledger — a plain-language, auditable record of material AI use, safeguards, and accountability.
What is the risk profile?
High-risk and illiquid. There may be no liquidity, no repayment, and no return. Total loss is possible. Independent professional advice is encouraged.
What happens after submitting interest?
A request is routed using the Request ID, followed by asynchronous fit screening. If aligned, a controlled packet (structure + notes + diligence checklist) may be shared under NDA.
