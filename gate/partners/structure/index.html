Valuation logic

Four layers of “value” in the early stages

Clarity over hype

Noetfield distinguishes between internal vision, conversational bands, contractual anchors in Partner Notes, and external market pricing. This avoids mixing storytelling with legal commitments.

  • 1. Vision / Mental valuation. The long-term potential if the Trust Ledger and Trust Brief pattern becomes a standard for boards and regulators. This is directional and internal – not written into any document.
  • 2. Verbal band (conversation-only). The range used in discussions with partners. For this phase, the verbal band is in the 6–8M CAD pre-money area, while being explicit that this is working and non-binding.
  • 3. Nominal / Contractual anchors. For Partner Notes, a concrete valuation cap (e.g. 6.6M CAD pre-money) and a 30% discount to future priced rounds are written into the documents. These anchors affect conversion economics, not today’s external “headline valuation.”
  • 4. Realised market valuation. The first time a professional investor prices an equity round (for example, a seed round at 12M CAD pre-money). From that point on, this becomes the reference valuation until the next priced round.
In short: Partner Notes use a cap and discount as an economic guardrail for early backers, but the “real” valuation only arrives when a third-party investor leads a priced equity round.
How this aligns with governance

Separating internal vision, conversational bands, contractual caps, and realised market prices mirrors how good governance separates narrative from formal obligations.

It allows early partners to understand the logic and participate on clear terms, without over-optimising or locking in a valuation too early for a governance product that is still in pilot mode.

Capital path

Illustrative path to the first 1M CAD of capital

Working example

The numbers below are illustrative. They are designed to show the shape of dilution and ownership if Noetfield raises approximately 1M CAD across an initial Partner Notes pool and a first priced equity round.

Phase 1 – Partner Notes (current phase)

  • Target: around 300k CAD of high-risk Partner Notes.
  • Working economics:
    • Unsecured Partner Notes, illustrative 7% simple interest per year.
    • Illustrative term: 36 months.
    • 30% discount to the first third-party priced equity round.
    • Valuation cap: illustrative anchor around 6.6M CAD pre-money.
  • If conversion happens after roughly 2 years, a 300k CAD pool converts at about 342k CAD (principal + 14% accrued interest).
  • At a 6.6M cap, this equates to approximately ~5–5.5% ownership on a fully diluted basis for the entire Partner Notes pool.

Phase 2 – First priced equity round

  • Illustrative round: 700k CAD raised in a seed-style round.
  • Illustrative pre-money valuation: 12M CAD.
  • New investors post-money ownership: ~5–6% (700k / 12.7M post-money, simplified).

Combining these two phases, Noetfield can plausibly reach 1M CAD of total capital raised while selling approximately 10–11% of the company, leaving roughly ~89–90% in founder / holding-company hands before creating an employee option pool.

All numbers are illustrative and simplified for clarity. Actual outcomes would depend on the final terms of each round, timing, aggregate size of the Partner Notes pool, interest accrual, and fully diluted capital structure at the time of conversion and pricing.

Design intent

Why this structure – and what it is optimised for

Governance-first design

Core design goals:

  • Preserve a clean operating cap table. Early capital is consolidated through a separate holding spine and Partner Notes, allowing Noetfield Systems Inc. to remain straightforward for future institutional rounds.
  • Reward early conviction without overcommitting equity. The combination of discount, cap, and simple interest gives early partners a clear upside profile while keeping total dilution modest for the first 1M of capital.
  • Respect the price-setting role of professional investors. The first major equity valuation is set by an external, lead investor in a priced round – not by an arbitrary internal number.
  • Stay compatible with future governance and regulatory expectations. The same discipline applied to AI governance is applied to capital structure: traceable logic, clean documentation, and clear separation between internal vision and contractual commitments.
How partners can use this page

This overview is intended to give potential partners enough structure to have a serious discussion with their internal teams, advisors, and co-decision makers.

Any actual participation would move from this working view to a specific term sheet and definitive documentation tailored to jurisdiction, profile, and regulatory requirements. For headline terms and Partner Notes tiers, partners should refer to the Founding Partner Notes overview.
