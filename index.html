Why boards cannot ignore AI governance anymore

AI is already embedded in credit decisions, pricing, operations, and safety-critical processes. Regulators and investors are starting to ask not just what you built, but how you governed it – and who is on record when decisions were made.

Emerging regulations and guidance, from the EU AI Act to sector supervisors, expect documented oversight of high-risk AI and automation.
Critical AI decisions are scattered across tickets, logs, chat threads, and slide decks – unreadable at board level and impossible to reconstruct under pressure.
Without a short, auditable history of key AI decisions, organisations face unanswerable questions after an incident, investigation, or audit.
Boards carry fiduciary and reputational risk when AI goes wrong, even if the evidence lives deep inside technical systems and never reaches them.

The Trust Ledger: a short, auditable history of your critical AI decisions

Technical teams keep detailed logs. The Trust Ledger is something different: a disciplined record of a few high-stakes AI and automation decisions – written in plain language and structured so boards, auditors, and regulators can actually work with it.

Decisions

Which AI systems, models, and use cases matter most for your organisation, and what decisions were taken about how they are used.

Data & Safeguards

What data they rely on, how they are controlled in production, and which safeguards and checks exist in practice.

Accountability

Who made the call, how dissent and risk were handled, and when the board or its committees were formally brought into the loop.

Example Trust Ledger entry

System / Use case: AI-based scoring for retail credit applications.
Decision: Approve use of model for applications up to $50k, with human review required above threshold.
Data: 5 years of historical applications and performance data, with excluded variables documented.
Safeguards: Quarterly bias checks, override logging, and independent model validation before major changes.
Board touchpoint: Risk Committee briefing in Q2; summary entry attached to minutes and annual risk report.
