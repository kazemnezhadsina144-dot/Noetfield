Why now

Why boards cannot treat AI as just another IT project

In many institutions, AI and automation have already moved into core processes – credit, pricing, onboarding, trading, triage. Yet the record of how those systems are governed is often scattered across model cards, emails, tickets, and decks. That leaves boards and regulators exposed.

Regulatory pressure
Supervisors want governance, not slogans

New AI rules and existing risk frameworks are converging on one question: how are material AI decisions governed in practice? Not just “do you use AI”, but “who decides, on what evidence, with which safeguards”.

Scattered artefacts
Important evidence is everywhere and nowhere

Model documentation, vendor contracts, monitoring dashboards, and incident logs rarely live in one place. When questions arise, stewards must reconstruct a governance trail from fragments.

Unclear accountability
It is not obvious who “owns” each AI decision

Responsibility for AI-enabled processes is often split across product, technology, and risk. Without a clear Ledger, it is difficult to show who approved thresholds, overrides, and safeguards.

Artifact

The Noetfield Trust Ledger – a compact record of AI decisions

The Trust Ledger is the central artefact: a structured, human-readable record of critical AI decisions, their safeguards, and their ownership. It does not try to mirror every log or metric. Instead, it surfaces the decisions that matter for boards, regulators, and internal assurance.

Decisions
What decisions were taken, and why

Each entry captures the decision context, the role of AI, and the rationale – in language that board members and supervisors can understand and question.

Safeguards & evidence
How the system is controlled in practice

Entries link to the artefacts that show the system is under control – from monitoring thresholds and override rules to incident logs and fairness checks.

Roles & thresholds
Who is accountable, at which levels

The Ledger clarifies who owns each decision, which committees are involved, and what “red lines” or escalation paths exist when AI behaviour drifts.

FAQ

Common questions from boards and executives

Do we need a large AI programme before talking to Noetfield?
No. Many organisations start with a few AI-enabled processes embedded in existing systems or vendor tools. What matters is that some of these already influence material decisions. The Trust Brief helps identify and govern them.
Is this an audit or a certification?
The Trust Brief is an advisory, diagnostic engagement. It does not produce a formal certification. Instead, it prepares you for audits, supervisory reviews, and internal assurance by clarifying what matters most and how it is currently governed.
How does Noetfield fit with our existing risk frameworks?
Noetfield is designed to sit on top of existing risk and control frameworks – model risk, operational risk, conduct, technology risk. The Trust Ledger maps entries to those structures so that AI governance is integrated, not separate.
Who should be in the room for a Trust Brief?
Typically a small cross-functional group: an executive sponsor (often CRO/COO/CDO), one or two model or product owners, and representation from risk or internal audit. Boards and committees then receive the outputs in a format they can own.
