Offering

Two connected pieces: Trust Brief and Trust Ledger

A focused entry pilot, and a repeatable governance artefact for boards, auditors, and supervisors.

Entry pilot
Noetfield AI Trust Brief (6 weeks)

A time-boxed engagement that maps material AI use, highlights risks and safeguards, and delivers a board-ready AI Trust Brief plus a first Trust Ledger sample.

  • 6–10 page AI Trust Brief in plain language.
  • 1–2 page Trust Ledger sample for critical AI systems.
  • 5–7 priority actions for the next 12 months.
Ongoing artefact
Noetfield Trust Ledger

A curated, plain-language record of the most critical AI decisions – how they were approved, what safeguards they rely on, and who is accountable – designed to sit above existing controls.

  • Focus on 3–10 material AI decisions, not every model.
  • Connects documentation, controls, and incidents into one history.
  • Built for boards, internal audit, and regulators.

Why now

From “Do you use AI?” to “How is it governed?”

Boards and funders are shifting from inventories to accountability: decision rights, safeguards, monitoring, and evidence that can be revisited over time.

Common gap
Fragments exist, but no narrative

Most organisations already have fragments: policies, vendor docs, logs, dashboards, model notes, tickets, and minutes. The gap is a coherent record that connects them.

Noetfield connects fragments into a short narrative that boards and supervisors can trust and reuse.
