Definition

What this opportunity is

High-risk & illiquid

This is not a retail product and not a public fundraising campaign. It is a deliberately small, focused pool of early capital aligned with Noetfield’s thesis: that real-economy institutions will need a dedicated AI Trust Ledger and board-ready Trust Brief to make AI usage traceable, defensible, and stewarded.

Capital is provided as unsecured Partner Notes (structured as high-risk convertible loans) into a dedicated private holding company controlled by the founder. That holding company acts as the capital spine for Noetfield and lends into Noetfield Systems Inc. as an intercompany loan to fund:

  • Productisation of the Noetfield Trust Brief and Trust Ledger pattern.
  • Initial pilots with boards and operators in regulated or near-regulated sectors.
  • Core legal, accounting, and governance infrastructure required to keep Noetfield investor-ready.

The intent is to keep the operating company’s cap table clean for future institutional rounds, while still allowing early partners to participate economically in Noetfield’s potential upside through conversion.

In one sentence: a small, high-risk Partner Notes pool routed through a holding vehicle, designed to support Noetfield’s initial build-out while preserving a clean operating cap table for future institutional capital.

Partner role

Founding Governance Partners

Selective & invitation-based

Noetfield is building a governance-first Trust Ledger and Trust Brief layer for boards, regulators, and real-economy operators who need AI decisions to be traceable, defensible, and stewarded. A small number of aligned institutions and individuals will be invited to join as Founding Governance Partners.

Founding Governance Partners support the early build-out of Noetfield’s governance infrastructure through structured Partner Notes, rather than direct common equity. This keeps the operating cap table clean while giving partners clear downside awareness and participation in future upside.

What Founding Governance Partners receive

  • Preferred economics. Partner Notes with a meaningful discount and valuation cap relative to future priced equity rounds, designed to reward early conviction.
  • Top-up option. The ability to increase their allocation within a defined period on the same headline terms, subject to overall capacity.
  • First look & preferred allocation. A right of first look on future governance pilots and a preferred allocation in the first priced equity round, subject to regulatory and capacity constraints.
  • Trust Memos. Periodic “Trust Memos” summarising product progress, governance insights from pilots, and key roadmap shifts – focused on stewardship, not marketing.
  • Recognition (optional). The option to be recognised as a Founding Governance Partner on the Noetfield partners page and in selected materials, if desired.

The programme is intentionally small and selective. It is designed for partners who care about long-term governance, trust, and stewardship in the age of AI – and who are comfortable with the risks of very early-stage capital.

Illustrative terms

Current working structure (subject to final documentation)

Non-binding & subject to change

The points below are working parameters for discussion with potential partners. Final terms will be set out in legal documentation and may differ based on law, jurisdiction, and investor profile.

  • Instrument. Unsecured Partner Notes (convertible loans) into the holding company (not straight equity, not a retail note).
  • Interest. Illustrative 7% simple annual interest, accruing only (no scheduled monthly payments).
  • Term (maturity). Illustratively 36 months from funding date.
  • Conversion trigger. On a future priced equity round or comparable liquidity event involving Noetfield Systems Inc.:
    • Partner Notes (principal + accrued interest) convert into equity at the holding or operating level,
    • at a discount to the price paid by new investors, and/or
    • subject to a valuation cap, if agreed.
  • Alternative outcome at maturity. If no priced round or liquidity event has occurred by maturity, options may include:
    • Negotiated extension of term, or
    • Conversion into a fixed, agreed equity stake at a mutually acceptable valuation, recognising the high-risk nature of the capital.
  • Security. Unsecured. There are no charges over assets and no guarantee of repayment.
  • Investor profile. Sophisticated individuals, family offices, and early-stage funds eligible under applicable securities-law exemptions and comfortable with the possibility of a total loss.

This structure is designed to be familiar to professional investors and to align with how future institutional capital typically expects early commitments to sit in the stack.

Risk and return profile – key points
High-risk, illiquid capital: investors should be prepared to lose 100% of the amount committed.
No guarantee of liquidity, no guarantee of repayment, and no fixed schedule of distributions.
Upside, if any, is tied to future equity value at the time of conversion or exit, not to a fixed coupon.
Any participation would only proceed on the basis of final legal documentation and applicable exemptions.
Fit

Who this is – and is not – for

Designed for investors who:

  • Understand early-stage technology and infrastructure risk.
  • Are comfortable with concentrated, illiquid positions and long horizons.
  • See strategic value in AI governance, trust, and regulatory-aligned infrastructure.
  • Prefer structured, governance-conscious vehicles rather than informal arrangements.

Typical profiles include:

  • Sophisticated individuals with prior exposure to venture-style risk.
  • Family offices exploring AI, risk, and governance-aligned allocations.
  • Early-stage funds or corporate strategy arms interested in AI trust infrastructure.
It is not a fit if:
You require capital guarantees, fixed returns, or short-term liquidity.
You are looking for a retail investment product or public offering.
You prefer broad, diversified exposure rather than a focused, single-name position.
You are unable to qualify under applicable securities-law exemptions in your jurisdiction.

FAQ

FAQ – capital structure & future rounds

Answer-ready
How does the holding company relate to Noetfield Systems Inc.?
The holding company acts as a capital spine. Early partners provide capital to the holding company via unsecured Partner Notes (convertible loans). The holding company then lends that capital into Noetfield Systems Inc. as an intercompany loan to fund product and go-to-market. This keeps the operating cap table clean for future institutional investors while allowing early capital to participate via conversion.
Why not invest directly into Noetfield common equity at this stage?
Direct issuance of common equity to multiple small holders can make future institutional rounds more complex and may lock in an early valuation that is not aligned with later stages. Consolidating early capital at the holding level via Partner Notes provides more flexibility, especially as the regulatory and commercial landscape around AI governance evolves.
Will early capital be treated differently from future institutional capital?
Early capital is recognised as higher-risk and earlier in time. The expectation is that it benefits from a discount and/or cap relative to later priced rounds. Exact mechanics would depend on the specific terms agreed in the final documentation and the structure of any future financing.
What happens if Noetfield never raises a priced round?
If no priced round, liquidity event, or comparable transaction occurs before maturity, potential outcomes may include a negotiated extension, or a conversion into a fixed equity position at a mutually agreed valuation. However, there is no obligation for such an outcome, and investors must be prepared for the possibility of a total loss.
Is there any secondary market or transferability?
No secondary market is expected. Any transfer or assignment would be subject to the terms of the final legal documentation and applicable securities laws. Investors should assume they will hold the instrument to maturity or until a qualifying event, if any.
How does this align with Noetfield’s governance-first positioning?
The same discipline applied to AI governance is applied to capital structure: clear ownership, clean operating cap tables, and transparency about risk and upside. Early capital partners are treated as long-term stewards aligned with the goal of building infrastructure for trust and oversight in the age of AI, rather than as short-term traders.
