Partner Notes Program

Partner Notes – structured, high-risk early exposure for aligned capital.

The Noetfield Partner Notes Program is a small, invitation-only track for sophisticated backers who want early, concentrated exposure to Noetfield’s AI governance and Trust Ledger platform. Notes are unsecured, convertible, and tied directly to the first third-party priced equity round – with clear downside structure and disciplined upside.

This page sits under the Early Capital & Stewardship Partners track and summarises the working structure of the Partner Notes. For a broader view of the holding company and capital spine, see the Structure overview.

Structure

How the Partner Notes work

Convertible, governance-first

Partner Notes are unsecured, high-risk convertible instruments. They are designed for a small group of aligned partners who understand that early-stage governance infrastructure is binary: either a durable, defensible layer is built, or capital is lost.

Each Note carries simple interest, a fixed term, and converts automatically into equity in the first third-party priced round at a discount. Discount levels typically sit in the 25–30% range, with selective use of valuation caps and modest warrant-style kickers for larger, strategic tickets.

  • Unsecured, high-risk, illiquid Partner Note – not a deposit and not a savings product.
  • Simple annual interest, accrued – no monthly interest payments.
  • Automatic conversion into the first third-party priced equity round at a 25–30% discount.
  • Valuation caps used selectively on larger tickets, anchored closer to later institutional pricing.
  • For strategic partners, a warrant-style equity kicker may be included, subject to legal structuring.
In one sentence: the Partner Notes give sophisticated backers structured downside and disciplined upside tied to the first real equity price a professional investor is willing to pay for Noetfield – without forcing excessive dilution in the first 300k of capital.
Risk, suitability, and scope

Partner Notes are only suitable for investors who can afford to lose 100% of their capital and who are familiar with early-stage technology and infrastructure risk.

They are not designed for retail investors, for capital that needs near-term liquidity, or for anyone seeking guaranteed or low-volatility returns.

Illustrative mechanics

How the discount and cap work in practice

The Partner Notes are not priced as a fixed equity percentage. Instead, they convert into the same class of shares as the first third-party priced round, on more favourable economics than new cash investors.

  • If, for example, a professional investor leads a round at a 10M CAD pre-money valuation, new cash investors pay that price per share.
  • A Partner Note converts at a 25–30% discount to that price and, where a cap applies, at or below the agreed cap level (for example, around a 6M pre-money anchor for certain strategic tickets).
  • The exact number of shares depends on the total amount raised, the final valuation, and the fully diluted capital structure at that time.

This keeps the economics disciplined and aligned with independent price discovery, rather than fixing low standalone valuations for the notes that would force excessive dilution in the first 300k of capital.

Key principles

Across all tiers, the intent is consistent:

  • Protect the future cap table from being over-engineered at the note stage.
  • Respect the price-setting role of the first institutional or professional investors.
  • Reward early conviction with a clear 25–30% discount and, for larger tickets, selective caps and modest warrant kickers.
  • Keep documentation clean and compatible with future institutional capital and governance expectations.

FAQ

FAQ – Partner Notes in practice

Answer-ready
Is this a public solicitation or crowdfunding?
No. The information here is illustrative and high-level. Any actual Partner Note would only be offered to investors who qualify for relevant exemptions under applicable securities laws and would be documented in formal agreements reviewed by counsel. No general solicitation or crowdfunding is intended.
What kind of investor is a good fit?
Investors who are comfortable with early-stage technology and infrastructure risk, who understand that capital is illiquid and high-risk, and who are interested in the specific niche of AI governance and board-level trust infrastructure. For example, certain family offices, sophisticated individuals, or institutions with an innovation or stewardship mandate.
How many partners will you work with in this phase?
The Partner Notes Program is intentionally small. Noetfield prioritises quality of alignment over the number of participants and expects only a handful of partners at each tier, if any, with aggregate capacity around the low hundreds of thousands of CAD.
Why notes instead of direct equity now?
Partner Notes allow Noetfield to build the early governance layer, run pilots, and reach a more natural price discovery point with professional investors. They avoid fixing the full valuation too early or over-complicating the cap table at the pre-pilot stage, while still aligning early supporters with clear upside via discounts and, where appropriate, caps and warrants.
Do Partner Notes guarantee any specific return?
No. There are no guaranteed returns or liquidity commitments. Outcomes range from 0 (capital loss) to successful conversion into equity at a discount with additional upside if Noetfield grows and executes. Any scenario should be evaluated with independent legal, tax, and investment advice.
Can terms be customised for a specific institution?
Within the same core spine (simple interest, 25–30% discount, clean governance), certain parameters can be tuned for a strategic institution or family office, subject to legal advice and the need to keep the overall structure compatible with future institutional rounds and targeted dilution levels.
