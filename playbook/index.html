The 0–90 pattern

The goal is not perfect policy. The goal is to make AI use governable — with clear scope, accountable owners, controls, and proof.

Phase 1: Baseline
0–30 days
  • Material AI inventory (what runs in production; what influences decisions).
  • Owner map: accountable roles, escalation, approval boundaries.
  • Minimum control baseline (access, logs, data handling, retention).
  • Decision framing: what boards must explicitly approve vs delegate.
Phase 2: Evidence
31–60 days
  • Evidence map: what counts as proof and where it lives.
  • Standard artefact pack (configs, screenshots, exports, runbooks).
  • First Trust Ledger entries for highest-impact use cases.
  • Board memo draft with options and trade-offs.
Phase 3: Cadence
61–90 days
  • Governance cadence (monthly ledger update; quarterly board review).
  • Change control (prevent drift; document exceptions).
  • Implementation routing (internal team or partners).
  • Incident drill: reconstruct decisions after the fact.
Output: Board-ready package
deliverables
  • Control baseline with owners and evidence expectations.
  • Starter Trust Ledger entries (material use cases first).
  • Board memo: options, priorities, and decision record.
  • Implementation plan: how controls + evidence trails get shipped.

Procurement FAQ

Common questions from boards, legal, privacy, security, and procurement.

Is this a policy document? ▾

No. Policy is optional. The focus is governed artefacts: owners, controls, evidence expectations, decision records, and ledger entries that remain readable under scrutiny.

What do you need from us? ▾

A short intake, an accountable owner contact, and access to non-secret exports/screenshots for evidence posture. If deeper access is required, it is handled through a controlled engagement path after Gate triage.

How do you handle confidentiality? ▾

Public intake is non-confidential. Sensitive details are not submitted via public forms. Any confidential exchange is routed into a controlled engagement path after Gate triage.

Who owns the outputs? ▾

The organization owns its governance artefacts and internal records. Noetfield retains its underlying methodologies, schemas, and delivery system.

Can our partner/MSP implement controls? ▾

Yes. Route partners through Partners Gate. Implementation lanes require evidence discipline so changes remain provable and auditable.

What happens after 90 days? ▾

The target outcome is a stable cadence: periodic updates, drift prevention, change control, and ledger maintenance—typically transitioning into Trust Ledger operations.
