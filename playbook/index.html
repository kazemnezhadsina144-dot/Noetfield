Definition

What the Playbook is

Practical & governance-first

The Noetfield AI Governance & Trust Playbook is a compact implementation guide that helps small B2B teams and agencies bring order, documentation, and governance to the way they use AI — without standing up a full compliance department.

It assumes AI is already embedded in sales, marketing, delivery, or operations. The Playbook brings structure: what is in scope, what risks matter, how decisions are approved, which safeguards apply, and how it is captured in a way boards and enterprise clients can recognise as credible governance.

The language and templates are designed to remain compatible with Noetfield’s board-level Trust Brief and Trust Ledger pattern, so teams can scale into the same governance vocabulary over time.

In one sentence: a 0–90 day plan and template kit that makes AI usage defensible and answer-ready for clients, boards, and regulators.
Inside the Playbook
Core components at a glance

Each section includes prompts and example language that can be adapted to a team’s stack.

  • Scope & mandate — what “AI” means and who owns it.
  • AI risk map — legal, reputational, operational risks for small teams.
  • Use-case intake — approve AI use-cases before deployment.
  • Data & privacy guardrails — what can / cannot be sent to tools.
  • Vendor & tool due diligence — acceptable tools and evidence.
  • Incident & escalation — what happens when something goes wrong.
  • Board/client brief — a short non-technical governance explanation.
  • 0–90 day roadmap — a realistic sequence for adoption.

FAQ

FAQ – using the Playbook in practice

Answer-ready
Who is the AI Governance & Trust Playbook for?
For small B2B teams and agencies that already use AI and now need a credible, structured way to show AI is governed and documented.
Does the Playbook replace legal or compliance advice?
No. It provides structure and templates compatible with legal/risk expectations and can be reviewed with counsel.
How much time does it take to implement?
Structured as a 0–90 day pattern. Many teams establish a credible v1 within 30–45 days focused on a small number of high-impact use-cases.
How does this relate to the Trust Brief and Trust Ledger?
The Playbook is the small-team track using compatible concepts. Teams can later migrate into Trust Brief and Trust Ledger without starting from zero.
How is the Playbook delivered?
As a structured document (PDF + editable formats) with templates that can be integrated into existing tools and client documentation.
Can we use it with our own clients?
Yes. The Agency / Pro license is designed for reuse across multiple client engagements.
