Noetfield AI Governance & Trust Playbook

Board-safe, regulator-ready AI governance for small B2B teams and agencies – without a full-time compliance or legal team.

Why this exists

Modern B2B teams are already using AI across sales, marketing, product, and operations. What is usually missing is a simple, defensible way to show that AI usage is controlled, documented, and aligned with basic governance expectations.

The Playbook is designed for small teams that:

  • Sell into enterprise or regulated clients
  • Expect questions from boards, investors, or key accounts about AI risk
  • Want order and documentation without turning into a compliance department

What you get

The Noetfield AI Governance & Trust Playbook is a compact implementation guide (0–90 days) plus ready-to-use templates.

  • A step-by-step framework to make AI usage visible, assessed, and documented
  • Clear roles and responsibilities for AI decisions inside a small team
  • A practical path to move from ad-hoc experiments to a lightweight governance system

Core components

  1. Orientation & Mandate – scope, ownership, and objectives
  2. AI Risk Map – legal, reputational, and operational risk structure for small teams
  3. AI Use-Case Intake Framework – how to evaluate AI use-cases before deployment
  4. Data, Privacy & Confidentiality Guardrails – what can be sent to AI systems, and under which conditions
  5. Vendor & Tool Due Diligence Checklist – a consistent way to review AI tools and vendors
  6. AI Policy Starter Pack – simple, editable policies (acceptable use, human-in-the-loop, incident)
  7. Incident & Escalation Playbook – what to do when something goes wrong
  8. Board & Stakeholder Reporting Template – a short, clear update format for boards and key clients
  9. 0–90 Day Implementation Roadmap – a realistic execution plan for small teams
  10. Living Documents Appendix – guidance on updates, versions, and maintenance

Templates included

The Playbook ships with editable templates that can be adapted to the current stack:

  • AI Use-Case Intake Form (Lite)
  • AI Risk Register for small teams
  • Vendor Due Diligence Checklist for AI tools
  • Board-Ready AI Governance Brief (1–2 pages)
  • Simple AI Policy v1.0 (editable starter)

Who it is for

Designed for:

  • B2B SaaS founders and operators
  • Marketing, analytics, and service agencies using AI with client data
  • Small teams preparing to sell into larger, more risk-sensitive customers

Most suitable when:

  • AI is already in use, but policies and documentation are minimal or fragmented
  • There is no full-time compliance officer, but governance questions are emerging
  • The team wants something practical that can be implemented within 30–90 days

Outcomes

After implementing the Playbook, a small team can:

  • Show that AI usage is visible, owned, and structured
  • Point to a simple set of documents when boards, investors, or enterprise clients ask about AI risk
  • Reduce the chance of unpleasant surprises around data, privacy, or misuse
  • Use AI more confidently, knowing there is a basic governance layer in place
