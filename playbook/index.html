Definition

What the Playbook is

Practical & governance-first

The Noetfield AI Governance & Trust Playbook is a compact implementation guide that helps small B2B teams and agencies bring order, documentation, and governance to the way they use AI – without standing up a full compliance department or legal function.

It assumes that AI is already embedded in sales, marketing, delivery, or operations. The Playbook brings structure: which uses are in scope, what risks matter, how decisions are approved, which safeguards apply, and how all of this is captured in a way that boards, investors, and enterprise clients can recognise as credible governance rather than experimentation.

Instead of another generic framework, the Playbook gives you a focused set of moves, templates, and example language to make AI usage visible, owned, and traceable – in a way aligned with Noetfield’s board-level Trust Brief and Trust Ledger pattern, so you can grow into the same governance language over time.

In one sentence: the Playbook is a structured, 0–90 day plan and template kit that makes your AI usage defensible and documented for clients, boards, and regulators, at the scale of a small B2B team.
Inside the Playbook
Core components at a glance

The Playbook is organised into a small number of sections. Each comes with prompts and example language you can adapt to your context.

  • Scope & mandate – what “AI” means in your context and who owns it.
  • AI risk map – legal, reputational, and operational risks tailored to small teams.
  • Use-case intake – vet and approve AI use-cases before deployment.
  • Data & privacy guardrails – what can and cannot be sent to AI tools.
  • Vendor & tool due diligence – how you decide which AI tools are acceptable.
  • Incident & escalation patterns – what happens when something goes wrong.
  • Board/client brief – a short, non-technical explanation of your AI governance.
  • 0–90 day roadmap – a realistic implementation sequence.

Templates

Templates that ship with the Playbook

The Playbook includes editable templates (for example doc and sheet formats) that you can adapt to your stack:

  • AI use-case intake form (lite) – capture purpose, data, model/tool, and risk notes for each use-case.
  • AI risk register for small teams – a simple way to track key AI-related risks and mitigations.
  • Vendor & tool due diligence checklist – what to ask before adopting an AI tool or API.
  • Board / client-ready AI governance brief – a 1–2 page summary of how you govern AI.
  • Simple AI policy v1.0 – an editable starter policy covering acceptable use, data, and human-in-the-loop.

These are designed to be minimal but credible – something a risk-conscious client or investor can recognise as real governance, not just marketing language or tool lists.

FAQ

FAQ – using the Playbook in practice

Answer-ready
Who is the AI Governance & Trust Playbook for?
It is designed for small B2B teams and agencies that already use AI in their work – for example in marketing, sales, analytics, content, or decision support – and now need a credible, structured way to show that AI usage is governed, documented, and aligned with basic risk expectations.
Does the Playbook replace legal or compliance advice?
No. The Playbook does not provide legal advice and does not replace formal compliance functions. It gives structure, language, and templates so you can organise your AI governance in a way that is compatible with legal, risk, and audit expectations, and can be reviewed with internal or external counsel.
How much time does it take to implement?
The Playbook is structured around a 0–90 day pattern. Many teams can establish a first, credible version of their AI governance layer within 30–45 days by focusing on a small number of high-impact use-cases, then iterate from there.
How does this relate to the Noetfield Trust Brief and Trust Ledger?
The Trust Brief and Trust Ledger focus on board-level AI governance for larger organisations. The Playbook is a parallel track for smaller B2B teams and agencies, using compatible concepts but scoped to your scale. As you grow or face higher regulatory pressure, the same ideas can be extended into a full Trust Brief and Trust Ledger engagement.
How is the Playbook delivered?
It is delivered as a structured document (PDF and editable formats) with accompanying templates. You can adapt the language to your context, integrate the templates into your existing tools, and use the brief as part of client, investor, or board communications.
Can we use the Playbook with our own clients?
Yes. The Agency / Pro licence is designed exactly for that: to let agencies and advisors use the Playbook and templates as part of their work with multiple clients, while keeping the underlying governance pattern consistent and recognisable.
