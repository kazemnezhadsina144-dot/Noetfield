Trust Brief

A six-week pilot that delivers a board-ready AI Trust Brief and Trust Ledger sample.

The Noetfield AI Trust Brief is a governance-first, six-week engagement that maps where AI is materially used in your work, highlights key risks and safeguards, and delivers one board-ready AI Trust Brief plus a sample Trust Ledger page – so AI decisions, responsibilities, and next steps are clear.

Who it is for

Boards, executives, and stewards under AI pressure

The Noetfield AI Trust Brief is designed for board chairs, executive directors, CEOs, and risk, audit, or compliance leaders who:

  • Are already using AI, or planning to use it soon, in ways that affect customers, money, or critical decisions.
  • Feel growing pressure from boards, funders, or regulators to show that AI usage is under control.
  • Want a clear, independent view of AI risks and safeguards – without a long consulting project.

Typical organisations include:

  • Credit unions and financial co-operatives
  • Nonprofits and foundations
  • Indigenous and community economic organisations
  • Mid-size companies with emerging AI use
Outcome

What you receive in 6 weeks

By the end of the pilot, your organisation receives:

  • A board-ready AI Trust Brief (6–10 pages) in plain language, mapping where AI is used today and where it is planned, the associated risks, current safeguards, and priority actions.
  • A sample Trust Ledger page (1–2 pages) that shows how critical AI systems and decisions can be documented going forward.

These give your board, funders, and regulators a clear picture of:

  • Where AI is used today and where it is planned.
  • Key risks, gaps, and dependencies.
  • Existing safeguards, controls, and accountable roles.
  • 5–7 priority actions for the next 12 months.
Standards

Aligned with emerging AI governance standards

The AI Trust Brief does not attempt to “certify” your organisation. Instead, it creates a practical, board-ready view of material AI use that can be read alongside emerging standards and frameworks.

The structure is designed to be compatible with risk-based AI rules and common governance frameworks (for example ISO-style management systems and NIST-style risk management approaches), so that your existing compliance and legal teams can integrate it into their own work without starting from zero.

Process

How the six-week pilot works

The Trust Brief is intentionally time-boxed. It focuses on a manageable number of AI systems and decisions so that you get a concrete, board-ready outcome in six weeks.

  1. Week 1 – Kickoff and intake
    60–90 minute kickoff with key stakeholders, agreement on scope and systems in focus, and secure collection of relevant policies, contracts, logs, and AI-related artefacts.
  2. Weeks 2–3 – Interviews and mapping
    2–3 structured conversations with people who own or operate AI-related tools, and a first map of AI use, risks, and existing safeguards.
  3. Week 4 – Synthesis and draft Brief
    Noetfield consolidates findings into a draft AI Trust Brief and a first Trust Ledger sample.
  4. Week 5 – Review and refine
    Joint review session to validate facts, language, and priorities, aligned with your board and regulatory context.
  5. Week 6 – Final delivery
    Final AI Trust Brief (PDF) and Trust Ledger sample, with an optional short presentation to leadership or a board delegate. All work can be delivered remotely.

Why now

Boards and funders are starting to ask simple questions

Across sectors, boards, funders, and regulators are beginning to ask:

  • Where are we using AI in ways that move risk, money, or customers?
  • What could go wrong if those systems fail or behave unexpectedly?
  • Who is accountable, and what safeguards exist?
  • How do we know those safeguards are actually monitored and enforced?
  • Where is this written down in a way we can revisit over time?

Most organisations do not yet have a short, structured document that answers these questions. The Noetfield AI Trust Brief is a focused way to move from ad-hoc conversations to one coherent view of AI risk and governance – without over-building or over-spending.

FAQ

Using the AI Trust Brief in practice

Who is the AI Trust Brief for?
It is designed for boards, executives, and risk leaders in organisations that already use AI in their work – or will soon – and now need a credible, structured way to show that AI usage is governed, documented, and aligned with basic risk expectations.
Does the Trust Brief replace legal or compliance advice?
No. The Trust Brief does not provide legal advice and does not replace formal compliance functions. It gives structure, language, and a baseline record that can be reviewed with internal or external counsel, auditors, or regulators.
How does it connect to the Trust Ledger?
The pilot produces a first Trust Ledger sample for one or two material AI systems. This acts as a template for expanding coverage over time or moving into a fuller Noetfield Trust Ledger engagement once the organisation is ready.
Can the pilot be delivered remotely?
Yes. All interviews, reviews, and briefings can be delivered remotely, with materials shared securely. In-person sessions are possible by separate arrangement where useful.
