What it is

A time-boxed, governance-first AI review

The Trust Brief is not an open-ended consulting engagement. It is a 6-week, time-boxed review that focuses on a small number of AI and automation decisions that actually move risk, money, or customers – and makes them legible to boards and committees.

The output is a board-ready report and a set of initial Trust Ledger entries. It gives stewards a concrete starting point for AI governance, without asking them to absorb every model card, dashboard, and ticket.

Phasing

How the 6 weeks unfold

Weeks 1–2
Inventory & interviews

Identify AI and automation that is capable of moving money, affecting customers, or triggering regulatory attention. Short, focused interviews across product, risk, and technology – no long workshops.

Weeks 2–3
Materiality & selection

Prioritise 3–5 decisions and use cases where governance needs to be explicit at board or committee level. Align on what “material” means in your context.

Weeks 3–5
Draft the first Ledger entries

Translate technical artefacts – documentation, dashboards, incidents – into plain-language Trust Ledger entries. Map gaps in safeguards, ownership, and evidence.

Weeks 5–6
Board-ready brief & options

Deliver a concise report and walk-through tailored for boards and executives, with options for how to continue: extend the Ledger, update policies, or set new thresholds for AI decisions.

Outcomes

What boards and executives walk away with

At the end of the Trust Brief, your organisation has a shared, governance-level view of AI – not a pile of slides. The emphasis is on traceable decisions and accountable roles.

  • A shortlist of AI and automation use cases that are actually material.
  • Initial Trust Ledger entries for the top 3–5 decisions, ready to attach to board packs and audit files.
  • A map of current safeguards and gaps – from monitoring thresholds to ownership.
  • Practical options for next steps that fit your regulatory and organisational context.
Board-facing checklist
Can we name our top 3–5 AI decisions that move risk, money, or customers?
Do we know what data and safeguards those systems rely on?
Is ownership for those decisions clearly tied to named roles?
If a regulator asked tomorrow, could we show how those decisions were governed?

FAQ

Trust Brief in practice

Who is the Trust Brief for?
The Trust Brief is designed for organisations that already have AI or automation in production – especially in financial services, infrastructure, utilities, or other real-economy sectors – and need a governance-level view before regulation or incidents force the issue.
How much time does it require from internal teams?
Most of the internal load is concentrated in short interviews, document access, and one or two review sessions. The aim is to keep time demands reasonable for senior leaders while still grounding the work in real systems.
Is this an audit or a certification?
No. The Trust Brief is an advisory, diagnostic engagement. It does not produce a formal certification. Instead, it prepares your organisation for audits, supervisory reviews, and internal assurance work by clarifying what matters most and how it is currently governed.
What happens after the Trust Brief?
Options typically include extending the Trust Ledger to additional decisions, updating AI and risk policies, or designing a lighter-weight reporting rhythm for boards and committees. The next step is tailored to your risk appetite and regulatory environment.
