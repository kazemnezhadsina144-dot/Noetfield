AI Trust Brief – 6-Week Pilot

The Noetfield AI Trust Brief is a focused, six-week pilot that maps where AI touches your work, highlights key risks and safeguards, and delivers:

  • A 6–10 page, board-ready AI Trust Brief in plain language.
  • A 1–2 page Trust Ledger sample for one or two critical AI systems.

It is time-boxed by design: narrow scope, concrete artefacts, no open-ended consultancy.

Who this is for

The Trust Brief is designed for:

  • Board chairs, executive directors, CEOs.
  • Risk, audit, or compliance leaders (CRO, CCO, General Counsel).

Organisations that are a good fit:

  • Already use AI (or will soon) in ways that affect customers, money, or critical decisions.
  • Feel growing pressure from boards, funders, or regulators to show that AI use is under control.
  • Want an independent, structured view without a large consulting project.

Typical partners include:

  • Credit unions and financial co-operatives.
  • Nonprofits and foundations.
  • Indigenous and community economic organisations.
  • Mid-size companies with emerging AI use.

What you receive in 6 weeks

1. AI Trust Brief (6–10 pages)

  • Where AI is used today and where it is planned.
  • Key risks, gaps, and dependencies.
  • Existing safeguards, controls, and accountable roles.
  • 5–7 priority actions for the next 12 months.

2. Trust Ledger sample (1–2 pages)

  • A condensed Ledger view of one or two critical AI systems and decisions.
  • Written in board-level language, with references to underlying artefacts.

6-week process

The pilot is structured into five stages over six weeks:

Week 1 – Kickoff and intake

  • 60–90 minute kickoff with key stakeholders.
  • Agree scope and systems in focus.
  • Collect relevant policies, contracts, logs, and AI-related artefacts securely.

Weeks 2–3 – Interviews and mapping

  • 2–3 structured conversations with system owners and operators.
  • First map of AI usage, risks, and existing safeguards.

Week 4 – Synthesis and draft Brief

  • Noetfield consolidates findings into a draft AI Trust Brief and draft Trust Ledger sample.

Week 5 – Review and refine

  • Joint review session to validate facts, language, and priorities, aligned with board and regulatory context.

Week 6 – Final delivery

  • Final AI Trust Brief (PDF) and Trust Ledger sample.
  • Optional short presentation to leadership or a board delegate.
  • All work can be delivered remotely.

How the Trust Brief connects to the Trust Ledger

The Trust Brief is the entry point into the Trust Ledger pattern:

  • It identifies where AI decisions are truly material for your organisation.
  • It drafts the first Ledger entries for one or two critical systems.
  • It prepares you to extend Ledger coverage, update policies and thresholds, and embed a repeatable reporting rhythm.
