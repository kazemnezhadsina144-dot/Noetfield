What it is

The Trust Ledger is the operational backbone that turns AI governance from “documents” into a living system: every claim is tied to evidence, every control has an owner, and every material use case has a decision trail.

Outcome
Board clarity
Executives see what AI does, who owns it, and what can go wrong.
Outcome
Evidence discipline
Controls and claims are backed by exports, logs, configs, and approvals.
Outcome
Lower drift
Policies stay aligned with real system behavior as tools and teams change.
Outcome
Audit survivability
Auditors can follow the proof trail without tribal knowledge.
What a ledger entry contains

A single “entry” is a governed snapshot of one material AI use case.

Field What it answers Proof
Use case What AI does, where it runs, who uses it, and what it affects. System mapping, vendor docs, app inventory.
Material impact Why this matters (money, safety, rights, decisions, public risk). Decision memo, risk statement, board thresholds.
Risks Failure modes: privacy, security, bias, fraud, IP, operational risk. Risk notes + incidents + test results (when available).
Controls What prevents/limits harm: access, DLP, logging, review, approvals. Config exports, screenshots, policy references, logs.
Owners Who is accountable; who approves changes; who maintains evidence. RACI, approvals, change control record.
Evidence links Where the proof lives and how it stays readable over time. Links to sanitized artefacts, log pipelines, audit bundles.
How it gets built

Typical path: diagnostic → baseline → ledger build → operational cadence.

  1. Map material use cases (where AI influences meaningful outcomes).
  2. Define control baseline (minimum viable governance with proofs).
  3. Create ledger entries (owners, risks, controls, evidence links).
  4. Set cadence (monthly/quarterly review + drift detection + change control).
