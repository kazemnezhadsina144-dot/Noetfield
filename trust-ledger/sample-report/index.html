How an AI Trust Ledger looks in practice.

This page collects two sample artefacts: a 1-page Trust Ledger entry and a short AI Trust Ledger board & regulator report. Both are designed to show how critical AI decisions, safeguards, and accountability can be recorded in a way that is answer-ready for boards and supervisors.

What these samples are designed to show

Enough structure for serious scrutiny, without drowning directors in technical detail.

The 1-page Trust Ledger entry compresses a single material AI decision – for example, a credit decision or triage threshold – into a concise record of decision, safeguards, accountability, and evidence. It is built to sit comfortably in a board pack or supervisory exchange.

The AI Trust Ledger report sample shows how those entries can be wrapped into a short narrative for boards and regulators: why the organisation uses AI, which decisions are in scope, how safeguards and ownership are structured, and how incidents or near-misses are surfaced.

In practice, clients adapt these templates to their own governance language, risk frameworks, and regulatory expectations – the pattern stays consistent, while details localise.

Outline of the AI Trust Ledger report sample

A condensed view of the sections your board, executives, and supervisors will typically expect.

Section overview
1. Purpose and scope
Why the report exists, which AI-enabled decisions are in scope, and how the organisation defines “material” AI use cases.
2. Context and regulatory environment
A short summary of the regulatory and supervisory landscape relevant to the organisation’s AI usage, including key expectations from boards and executives.
3. Inventory of material AI decisions
A curated list of AI-influenced decisions that move risk, money, or customers – the backbone for Trust Ledger entries.
4. Example Trust Ledger entries
Selected entries, each describing a specific AI decision, its safeguards, accountability, and links to underlying documentation and controls.
5. Governance, roles, and ownership
How responsibilities are allocated between model owners, risk owners, committees, and the board – and how the Trust Ledger sits above existing risk and control frameworks.
6. Safeguards, limits, and monitoring
The organisation’s approach to setting thresholds, overrides, human-in-the-loop steps, and automated rollbacks for material AI systems.
7. Incidents, near-misses, and lessons
How AI-related incidents and near-misses are captured, escalated, and used to update both controls and Trust Ledger entries over time.
8. Next steps and maturity path
A forward-looking view of how the organisation plans to extend and deepen its AI governance, including how often the Trust Ledger will be refreshed for the board and supervisors.
